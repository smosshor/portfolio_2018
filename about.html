About me

I am a product designer with hybrid interaction and visual design skills. I utilize a diverse set of user research methods, such as interviews, concept models, usability tests and data analysis, to build an understanding of user needs and desires, before digging deep into designing pixel-perfect interactive prototypes, and working with engineers to reach the finish line. I have worked with large-scale web apps, e-commerce websites, and social platforms to create and improve user experiences.

I graduated from Carnegie Mellon with a B.S. in Human-Computer Interaction and Decision Science, with a Minor in Learning Media. My interdisciplinary passion for design, technology and social science research come together in how I approach each project, considering the subtle behavioral changes that will influence how a user interacts with the brand, product or interface.

I'm based in San Francisco, and active in Ladies that UX, OpenIDEO and Girl Develop It. In my free time, I like to cook, laser-cut jewelry, hike, run long miles, and practice yoga.
