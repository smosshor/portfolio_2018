Dentist Link

Finding dental care when you're on Medicare is unarguably difficult- from simply finding a provider that takes your insurance to making sure it's possible to get there on public transporation. DentistLink is a free service designed by Arcora Foundation to help under-served persons on Medicare to locate Dental providers that take their insurance, and to support providers and social support workers in supporting them throughout the process.

March 2018- Present

I worked on all aspects of the design process, sprinting ahead of the development team to keep a fast paced project on track, from basic wireframes to high-fidelity prototypes.

Process

As a first step, I created a series of customer journey maps to best understand the multiple users involved in this space- patients, care coordinators, providers and Acora representatives. I compared these journey maps, as well as general design heuristics to the basic wireframes that were planned, in order to evaluate the existing plans.

Much of the core functionality of the product is in the dashboard- a patient database for all logged-in users that contains the appointment creation flow and helps users track their cases. I created a number of quick, low-fidelity mockups and iterated based on feedback from Acora, including dental provider experts.

I adapted Acora's brand identity guidelines for DentistLink, making the secondary blue the primary color in Dentist Link. I worked with a flat design framework to keep the look and feel simple and similar to other applications people have used.

User Testing

I planned and conducted a simple usability testing protocol and trained the Acora team to conduct with their network of users- patients, primary care providers, dental providers and social service providers.
